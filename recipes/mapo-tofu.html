Mapo Tofu

Mapo Tofu is a comforting Chinese dish featuring soft tofu in a savory, slightly spicy sauce with ground meat and aromatic seasonings, perfect for those who prefer a gentle heat.

Ingredients

  • 1 stalk green onion
  • 3 shallot
  • 2 dried chili pepper (increase amount to add more spice)
  • 2 clove garlic
  • 1 mini orange sweet pepper
  • 1 mini red sweet pepper
  • 1 lb soft tofu (or your preferred firmness)
  • 3 oz ground pork
  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 1.5 tbsp cornstarch
  • 2 tbsp water
  • 1 tbsp ground bean sauce
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 tbsp oyster sauce
  • 1 tbsp light soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp dark soy sauce
  • 1 tsp sugar
  • 0.50 tsp salt
  • 0.50 tsp chili sauce (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tsp sesame oil

Steps

  1. Mince the green onion and shallots, smash and mince the garlic, and dice the mini sweet peppers and dried chili peppers.
  2. Prepare the tofu by draining and rinsing it, then cut into bite-sized cubes, taking care to handle soft tofu gently so it doesnâ€™t break.
  3. Heat oil in a wok over medium-high heat, then stir-fry the garlic, shallots, and chilies until fragrant.
  4. Add the pork and cook until browned.
  5. Stir in the ground bean sauce, water, oyster sauce, soy sauces, sugar, salt, and chili sauce, then gently add the tofu and coat it with the sauce.
  6. Simmer briefly, then thicken the sauce with a cornstarch slurry, add the sweet peppers and half of the green onions, and finish with sesame oil.
  7. Plate, garnish with the remaining green onions, and serve.
